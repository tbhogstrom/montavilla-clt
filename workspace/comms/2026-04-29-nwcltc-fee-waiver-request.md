Dear NWCLTC Membership Team,

My name is Tyler Falcon and I'm the coordinator of the Montavilla Community Land Trust, a community-led initiative forming in Portland, Oregon's Montavilla neighborhood. I'm writing to apply for NWCLTC membership and to respectfully request a waiver or deferral of our first-year membership fee.

**Where we are:** MCLT has been formally organizing for approximately two weeks. We are working to acquire a 0.84-acre commercial site at 7700 SE Stark Street in Portland — currently listed at $5M, significantly overpriced — and place it under community land trust stewardship for regenerative agriculture, green infrastructure, and community-determined uses.

We are pre-incorporation and pre-revenue. We have not yet secured a fiscal sponsor or filed for 501(c)(3) status, though both are actively underway. To date, we have approximately 40 petition signatures, 20 member signups, and $1,175 in individual pledges. We have a meeting with Proud Ground's executive director scheduled for next week, and we've begun outreach to the national CLT network through Grounded Solutions.

**Why we want to join now:** We believe NWCLTC membership would be most valuable to us at exactly this stage — during formation, when technical assistance, peer learning, and connections to experienced CLTs matter the most. We don't want to wait until we're established to join the coalition; we want to be part of it as we build.

**Our financial reality:** We are applying to the Portland Clean Energy Fund (PCEF) for a $200,000 planning grant, with a May 27 deadline. We're also preparing applications for USDA Local Food Promotion and Farmers Market Promotion programs (June 5 deadline). We have no organizational revenue and are operating entirely on volunteer labor and small individual pledges. The $100 Start-up CLT membership fee is modest under normal circumstances, but in our current pre-revenue state, we're directing every available dollar toward incorporation costs, fiscal sponsorship, and grant application expenses.

**Our commitment:** We commit in writing to paying full Start-up CLT dues upon securing our first grant award or closing on our initial fundraising round — whichever comes first. As MCLT grows, we fully intend to upgrade to the appropriate membership tier. We want to be contributing members of this coalition for the long term, not just beneficiaries of its generosity at the start.

We would be grateful for either a full waiver of first-year dues or a deferral until we reach a revenue milestone. We're happy to provide any additional information about our initiative, our timeline, or our plans.

Thank you for considering this request. We're looking forward to being part of the NWCLTC community.

Sincerely,

Tyler Falcon
