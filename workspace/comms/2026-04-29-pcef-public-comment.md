Dear Portland Clean Energy Fund Committee,

Thank you for the opportunity to submit a public comment for tonight's meeting. My name is **[your name]** and I'm writing on behalf of the **Montavilla Community Land Trust** initiative — a neighborhood-led effort organizing around the future of 7700 SE Stark St and the Montavilla Farmers Market node at 79th & Stark.

Our work sits squarely inside PCEF's mandate. We are organizing community ownership of land in an East Portland corridor that has historically been underinvested in, with explicit goals that align with the Climate Investment Plan:

- **Urban food systems and regenerative agriculture** — preserving a 70+ vendor producers' market that is the neighborhood's anchor for fresh, locally grown food, and protecting space for a year-round community food hub.
- **Low-carbon, community-scale construction** — our build program is designed around mass timber and adaptive reuse rather than concrete-and-steel speculative development.
- **Community-led decision-making** — the CLT model puts land-use decisions in the hands of the residents who live with the outcome, not absentee owners or out-of-state speculators.
- **Green public space** — the parcel borders the existing PBOT-designated Montavilla Plaza, which already hosts a Vibrant Spaces–funded weeknight market.

We intend to apply to the **2026 Community Grant cycle (deadline May 27)** for a planning grant, and we are exploring fit with **Strategic Program 9** for the land-acquisition portion of the project. As an early-stage, community-led applicant working through a fiscal sponsor, we ask the Committee to keep two things in view as you deliberate tonight:

1. **Continued accessibility of the Community Grant program to small, emerging, neighborhood-rooted applicants** using fiscal sponsorship — these are exactly the groups PCEF was created to fund.
2. **Clarity in Strategic Program 9 about eligibility for community land ownership models** as the land-acquisition vehicle for urban-agriculture and food-systems work.

We would welcome a site visit from PCEF staff and any Committee members. Please reach me at info@montavillalandtrust.org

Thank you for your service.
