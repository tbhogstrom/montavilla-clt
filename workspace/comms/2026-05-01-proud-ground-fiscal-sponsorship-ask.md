Dear [ED first name],

Thank you again for the conversation [last week / on (date)] — it meant a lot that you made time to hear what we're building in Montavilla, and your perspective on the practical realities of CLT formation was exactly what we needed at this stage.

Following up on what we discussed, I'd like to formally request that **Proud Ground serve as the short-term fiscal sponsor for the Montavilla Community Land Trust's near-term grant applications**, beginning with our application to PCEF's 2026 Community Grants planning round (deadline May 27).

I want to be specific about the scope of the ask, because I know fiscal sponsorship can mean different things to different organizations.

### What we are asking Proud Ground to do

1. **Serve as fiscal sponsor on the PCEF planning grant application** ($200,000, 1-year planning grant under the Regenerative Agriculture & Green Infrastructure category). PCEF's rules make the fiscal sponsor the formal grantee, signatory of the grant agreement, and legally responsible for funds — Proud Ground would hold those obligations.
2. **Hold short-term donations** received in support of MCLT during this same pre-incorporation period (currently ~$1,175 in individual pledges, growing slowly). We would route donations through Proud Ground's existing donation infrastructure and they would be designated for MCLT's use.
3. **Potentially extend the same arrangement** to two additional concurrent applications already in our pipeline (USDA Local Food Promotion Program and USDA Farmers Market Promotion Program, both with June 5 deadlines) — though we are open to discussing whether to start with PCEF only and revisit the others.

### What we are NOT asking Proud Ground to do

- We are **not** asking Proud Ground to acquire, hold, or carry title to the 7700 SE Stark Street property, or to take on any real-estate liability in connection with the planning grant. The property work is feasibility and community planning at this stage, not acquisition. When MCLT eventually pursues land control, it will be MCLT — not Proud Ground — that holds the parcel.
- We are **not** asking Proud Ground to take on staff, become MCLT's long-term home, or commit to ongoing fiscal sponsorship beyond the period needed for MCLT to incorporate and obtain its own 501(c)(3) determination.

### The transition we are proposing

The PCEF planning grant scope explicitly includes MCLT's own organizational formation — bylaws, governance design, 501(c)(3) filing, board development. Once MCLT has its own determination letter (we anticipate within 12–18 months of grant award), **fiscal sponsorship would end and the relationship would shift to a technical assistance partnership only.** That's the long-term structure we want with Proud Ground regardless of how the fiscal sponsorship piece resolves: TA partner and trusted peer in the regional CLT ecosystem, not parent organization.

### Terms we are prepared to offer

- **Sponsor fee:** Up to PCEF's allowed maximum of 10% of the grant ($20,000 on a $200,000 award). We would discuss the appropriate rate with Proud Ground; the 10% cap is what PCEF permits, not necessarily what we are proposing as the rate.
- **Reporting division:** Proud Ground handles financial reporting to PCEF (quarterly); MCLT handles programmatic and performance reporting; both review jointly before submission.
- **Donation handling fee:** Whatever Proud Ground's standard rate is for sponsored donation handling — we'll defer to your existing practice.
- **Mutual termination terms:** A clean off-ramp for both sides if either party determines the arrangement isn't working, with reasonable transition language for any open grant funds.

### What we need from Proud Ground for the PCEF application

PCEF requires the following from any fiscal sponsor at the time of application:

- EIN
- Three years of audited financial statements
- Description of financial management practices and internal controls
- A designated contact (signatory authority for the grant agreement)

Submission deadline is May 27. Our internal target is to have the full draft ready by May 17 so the application can sit for a week before submission. **For that timeline to work, we'd need Proud Ground's confirmation by ~May 10.**

### Why we believe this is well-aligned for Proud Ground

- **The CLT model is already part of Proud Ground's PCEF track record.** Proud Ground's "2025 Net Zero Homes" grant ($2.65M, 3-year) and your prior 2024 PCEF 3 award ($2.5M, 3-year) have established the CLT model with PCEF reviewers. Adding Montavilla as the next CLT-model project in PCEF's planning-grant pipeline extends that work eastward into a neighborhood that has not previously had a CLT presence.
- **East Portland geography.** Montavilla sits on the 82nd Avenue corridor — a documented environmental justice priority area where Proud Ground does not currently have a project. This expands Proud Ground's geographic footprint into terrain that PCEF wants funded.
- **Bounded scope, low operational drag.** Planning-grant fiscal sponsorship — where Proud Ground is not acquiring property or staffing programs — is a relatively contained obligation. We are committed to being a low-friction sponsored entity: clear deliverables, on-time reporting, no scope creep into Proud Ground's own work.
