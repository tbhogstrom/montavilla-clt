## Disclosure 2: PCEF Planning Grant ($200K)

This is the bigger disclosure. Frame it as something that **serves MFM's interests without MFM carrying the work or the risk.**

> "There's a Portland Clean Energy Fund planning grant — up to $200,000, due May 27. We're scoping it as technical analysis of 7700 SE Stark: site assessment, building feasibility, community engagement design. The output would be exactly the kind of due-diligence work your board would want done before any decision about the market's future on this site. MCLT applies and carries the work. MFM benefits from the analysis. It's not contingent on your endorsement — but if you see overlap with questions your board is already asking, I'd want to know that."

**If Lisa asks who the fiscal sponsor is:** "We're still securing one — that's part of the Proud Ground conversation and other outreach we're doing this week."

**If Lisa asks what "technical analysis" means specifically:** Site assessment, broker price opinion, building-program feasibility (what could the site support physically), community engagement process design. The kind of homework that turns survey data into a site decision.

**If Lisa says "we've been wanting exactly this":** Mirror it. "That's what I was hoping to hear. What specific questions would be most useful for your board?" Do not overpromise — the grant hasn't been submitted, let alone awarded.

## Listening Prompts — Questions to Ask and Wait On

Ask these when the moment is right, not as a checklist. One or two good answers are worth more than five rushed ones.

1. **"What does your board need to see before they can make a long-term decision about the site?"**
   (This tells Tyler what analysis to prioritize in the PCEF scope.)

2. **"Is there a timeline your board is working against — anything that's creating urgency on your end?"**
   (Reveals whether MFM has deadline pressure Tyler doesn't know about.)

3. **"If someone handed you a site assessment and feasibility study for 7700 SE Stark tomorrow, what's the first question you'd want it to answer?"**
   (Directly shapes the PCEF application scope.)

4. **"How do you think about the relationship between the market and the neighborhood beyond your shoppers — the businesses on Stark, METBA, the broader community?"**
   (Opens the door to METBA and broader coalition dynamics without Tyler having to raise them.)

5. **"Is there anything I should know going into the Proud Ground conversation that you think matters?"**
   (Treats Lisa as a peer advisor, which she is.)
