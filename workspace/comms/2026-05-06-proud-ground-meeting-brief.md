## New Intel from the Lisa Meeting — What Proud Ground Should Know

### 1. MFM's lease at 7700 SE Stark is $0/year

MFM pays no rent for the site it has occupied for 20 years. Implications:

- Any CLT-anchored scenario that introduces *any* cost to MFM must deliver something MFM doesn't currently have — security of tenure, a permanent structure, year-round capacity.
- The value proposition of a CLT for MFM is not cost savings. It's permanence.
- This is relevant to how Proud Ground thinks about ground lease structures for commercial/community tenants vs. their usual residential model.

**How to raise it:** "MFM currently pays nothing for their lease at the site. So the CLT value proposition for them isn't cost savings — it's security of tenure and permanent infrastructure. That shapes how we'd structure any ground lease."

### 2. MFM has real capacity constraints

Lisa was candid: MFM does not have staff bandwidth to drive or operationally manage a technical planning process. Whatever scope MCLT proposes has to be something MFM can *receive*, not something they have to *run*.

**How to frame it:** "MFM is willing but stretched. They're a small nonprofit running a market with ~100 vendors on a part-time staff. MCLT absorbs the technical-planning load — site assessment, feasibility, community engagement — so MFM benefits from the analysis without having to carry it. That's why we need TA from Proud Ground: we're the entity doing the work."

### 3. Tyler offered MFM a board seat on MCLT

Tyler committed to Lisa that MFM would have a seat on the MCLT board. Governance intent — not yet codified in bylaws (Statton's territory).

**How to frame it:** "We've committed to reserving a board seat for MFM. It's part of the tripartite governance model we're designing — community members, institutional stakeholders, and public-interest representatives. Bylaws aren't drafted yet, but the commitment has been made."

**Why Proud Ground cares:** A CLT with the primary site tenant on its board is more credible than one operating independently. This is exactly the kind of stakeholder governance Proud Ground would want to see in a startup CLT.
