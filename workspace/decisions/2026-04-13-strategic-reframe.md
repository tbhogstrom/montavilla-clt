# Decision: Strategic Reframe — Community-First, Outcome-Open

**Date:** 2026-04-13
**Participants:** Statton (Founder), Maven (Campaign Director)
**Status:** APPROVED

---

## The Decision

The Montavilla CLT campaign will reframe from **"Save This Lot (for a farmers market)"** to **"The neighborhood decides what happens here."**

The CLT's role is to build a coalition, negotiate with the property owner, and execute whatever the community determines is the highest and best use — whether that's:

- A community commons with year-round market, green space, and services (the current prospectus vision)
- Affordable/low-income housing
- Mixed-use development with market and housing
- A market street or festival street model
- Something else entirely that emerges from community process

## Rationale

1. **Reddit feedback (78 comments)** showed the "save this lot for a farmers market" framing loses to Portland's pro-housing consensus. But the underlying energy — people care deeply about this neighborhood — is real.

2. **A community-decides framing neutralizes the housing argument.** We're not anti-housing. We're pro-community-voice. If the coalition determines housing is the right answer, then housing gets built. The point is that the decision belongs to Montavilla, not to an absentee Michigan owner or a speculative developer.

3. **This is what a CLT actually is.** Community land trusts exist to give communities democratic control over land use. Pre-determining the outcome before the community has been consulted is backwards.

4. **This framing is more fundable.** Funders and city agencies support community-driven planning processes. "Help us run a real community engagement process" is a grant-ready ask. "Help us buy a gravel lot for $5M" is not.

5. **This framing is more credible.** The biggest criticism was "you have no endorsements and no plan." A coalition-building, community-process approach doesn't need to have the answer on day one — it needs to have the right question and the right people in the room.

## What Changes

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "SAVE THIS LOT" | "OUR NEIGHBORHOOD, OUR DECISION" (or similar) |
| Pre-determined outcome (market commons) | Community-determined outcome |
| Asking for $5M | Asking people to join the conversation |
| Petition to "save" | Petition to "join the coalition" |
| Pledge = money for acquisition | Pledge = commitment to participate |
| Fighting the housing argument | Welcoming the housing conversation |
| Credibility through vision docs | Credibility through inclusive process |

## What Stays the Same

- The CLT as the vehicle (community ownership of land)
- The urgency (property is listed, clock is ticking)
- The coalition-building work (steering committee, MNA, market org)
- The need for legal structure, fiscal sponsorship, Proud Ground relationship
- Broker negotiation as a coalition activity

## What This Means for the Website

The current site leads with "SAVE THIS LOT" and presents the market commons as a fait accompli. This needs to evolve:

1. **Headline:** From "Save This Lot" → something like "Montavilla Decides" or "Our Neighborhood, Our Future"
2. **Vision section:** Present the market commons as ONE possibility, not THE plan. Add housing, mixed-use, and market-street as other options the community could pursue.
3. **Call to action:** From "pledge money to buy this lot" → "join the coalition shaping Montavilla's future"
4. **Prospectus:** Needs revision. Currently reads as a predetermined plan. Should read as a community process with multiple scenarios.
5. **Petition framing:** From "sign to save" → "sign to have a voice"

## What This Means for the Coalition

The coalition's first task is NOT to raise $5M. It's to:

1. Host community listening sessions (what does Montavilla want here?)
2. Engage the Montavilla Farmers Market org as a stakeholder (not the sole beneficiary)
3. Engage the MNA formally
4. Commission a broker price opinion (what is the lot actually worth?)
5. Research all options (acquire lot, alternative sites, street closure, mixed-use partnership)
6. THEN make a collective decision on direction
7. THEN fundraise/negotiate toward that direction

## Risk

The risk of an open-ended process is that it takes time, and the property could sell while we deliberate. Mitigations:
- Monitor the listing weekly (geekspice says no developer will pay $5M — we may have more time than we think)
- Explore option contract or letter of intent to buy time
- The Michigan owner has an inflated ask; the lot may sit for months
- Even if the lot sells to a developer, a strong coalition can negotiate community benefit agreements

## Statton's Words

> "My gut is telling me that there is community support for keeping the lot as greenspace and a farmers market focused lot. My feeling is we should still move forward with building a neighborhood coalition, but there is a lot of work to do on what the community wants. I want the coalition to get together and negotiate with the broker. If at the end housing gets built — that'll be a reflection of the will of the community."

This is the mission statement. The community decides.
