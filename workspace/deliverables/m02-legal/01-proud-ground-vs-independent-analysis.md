# Proud Ground Partnership vs. Independent 501(c)(3)

## Comparative Legal Analysis for the Montavilla Community Land Trust

**Prepared by:** Statton, Legal Counsel
**Date:** April 13, 2026
**Re:** Structural Options for the CLT Entity — 7700 SE Stark Street Acquisition

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The Montavilla Community Land Trust ("MCLT") must select a legal vehicle to acquire and hold the 0.84-acre parcel at 7700 SE Stark Street, Portland, Oregon (the "Property"). Two structural paths exist: (a) partnership with or fiscal sponsorship by Proud Ground, Portland's established Community Land Trust; or (b) formation of an independent Oregon nonprofit public benefit corporation with its own 501(c)(3) determination. **This analysis recommends Option B — formation of an independent 501(c)(3) — as the legally sound and strategically necessary path.**

---

## 2. Option A: Proud Ground Partnership

### 2.1 Proud Ground's Structure and Mission

Proud Ground (EIN: 93-1290320) is an Oregon 501(c)(3) nonprofit public benefit corporation founded in 1999 as the Portland Community Land Trust. It serves six counties in Oregon and SW Washington, stewarding 740+ households under 99-year ground leases.

**Critical finding:** Proud Ground's mission is narrowly defined around *permanently affordable homeownership*. Its IRS-approved exempt purpose, board structure (tripartite: homeowners, community/partner representatives, general public), and operational history are exclusively residential.

### 2.2 Partnership Models Considered

| Model | Description | Feasibility |
|---|---|---|
| **Fiscal Sponsorship** | Proud Ground holds donations and land on MCLT's behalf during formation | **Low** — Proud Ground's exempt purpose does not encompass commercial community space; accepting and holding a $5M commercial property could jeopardize its own tax-exempt status under IRC 501(c)(3). No precedent found. |
| **Project Partnership** | Proud Ground acquires the land and ground-leases to MCLT or the Market | **Low** — Same mission-scope problem. Proud Ground's board would need to approve a mission expansion, which would require IRS notification and potentially a new determination letter. |
| **Technical Assistance** | Proud Ground advises MCLT on ground lease structure, governance, and stewardship | **High** — No mission conflict. Proud Ground has deep institutional knowledge of CLT operations in Portland. This is the recommended form of engagement. |
| **Co-Stewardship** | Proud Ground co-stewards the land if MCLT later adds residential units | **Medium** — Possible for any future residential component only. Would require a formal inter-organizational agreement. |

### 2.3 Risks of the Proud Ground Partnership Path

1. **Mission Mismatch (HIGH RISK).** A farmers market, commissary kitchen, and community hub are commercial/community uses, not residential homeownership. Proud Ground accepting this project could trigger IRS scrutiny of its exempt purpose under IRC 501(c)(3) and Treas. Reg. 1.501(c)(3)-1(d)(1)(ii).

2. **Loss of Autonomy (MEDIUM RISK).** Under fiscal sponsorship, MCLT would not control its own governance, asset disposition, or ground lease terms. Proud Ground's board — not the Montavilla community — would hold fiduciary authority over the Property.

3. **Timeline Dependency (MEDIUM RISK).** Proud Ground's board cycle, internal review processes, and potential need for IRS consultation on mission scope would introduce delays incompatible with the urgency of the acquisition timeline.

## 3. Option B: Independent 501(c)(3) — Recommended

### 3.1 Formation Path

| Step | Action | Timeline | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Draft and file Articles of Incorporation with Oregon Secretary of State (ORS 65.047) | Week 1–2 | $50 filing fee |
| 2 | Adopt Bylaws with CLT-specific governance (tripartite board, community membership) | Week 2–3 | — |
| 3 | Apply for EIN (IRS Form SS-4) | Week 2 | Free |
| 4 | File IRS Form 1023 for 501(c)(3) determination | Week 3–6 | $600 filing fee |
| 5 | Register with Oregon DOJ Charitable Activities Section (ORS 128.610–128.750) | Upon formation | $20–$400 |
| 6 | Apply for Oregon property tax exemption (see separate ORS 307 analysis) | Upon acquisition | — |

### 3.2 Why Form 1023 (Not 1023-EZ)

IRS Form 1023-EZ is available only to organizations with projected gross receipts under $50,000/year and assets under $250,000. Upon acquisition of the Property (listed at $4,999,000), MCLT will far exceed these thresholds. **The full Form 1023 is required.** Current IRS processing time: 3–6 months.

**Interim Strategy:** MCLT may solicit donations during the pendency of its 1023 application. If approved, the determination is retroactive to the filing date of the Articles of Incorporation, provided the application is filed within 27 months (IRC 508(a); Treas. Reg. 1.508-1(a)(2)).

### 3.3 Advantages of Independent Formation

1. **Mission Alignment.** Articles of Incorporation and bylaws can be drafted specifically for a *commercial and community-use CLT* — a broader exempt purpose than Proud Ground's homeownership focus. The IRS has approved 501(c)(3) status for CLTs holding community facilities, markets, and shared commercial space under the "lessening the burdens of government" and "community development" rationales.

2. **Governance Autonomy.** MCLT's board will be accountable to Montavilla stakeholders: market vendors, neighborhood residents, and community partners. The tripartite board structure can be tailored to this project's constituencies rather than inheriting Proud Ground's homeowner-centric governance.

3. **Ground Lease Control.** MCLT drafts its own ground lease terms, including permitted uses (farmers market, commissary kitchen, community hub, commercial tenants like Portland Guitar Repair), transfer restrictions, and improvement ownership provisions. No need to negotiate within another organization's existing template.

4. **Grant Eligibility.** An independent CLT with its own 501(c)(3) determination can apply directly for USDA Farmers Market Promotion Program grants, CDBG funding, Meyer Memorial Trust grants, and CDFI financing. Some funders prefer direct grantees over fiscally sponsored projects.

5. **Property Tax Strategy.** An independent 501(c)(3) can pursue property tax exemption under ORS 307.130 (charitable institution exemption) on its own merits, without complicating another organization's tax status.

### 3.4 Risks of Independent Formation

1. **IRS Processing Time (MEDIUM RISK).** The 1023 application may take 3–6 months. Mitigation: file immediately upon adoption of Articles; use interim fiscal sponsor (not Proud Ground — a general-purpose fiscal sponsor like the Social Good Fund or a local community foundation) for early fundraising.

2. **Organizational Capacity (MEDIUM RISK).** A new nonprofit must build governance infrastructure from scratch: board recruitment, financial controls, insurance, annual reporting. Mitigation: recruit experienced board members; engage Proud Ground for technical assistance on CLT operations.

3. **Credibility Gap (LOW RISK).** Funders and the seller may view a new entity as less established. Mitigation: strong community petition (already underway), advisory board with recognized names, and partnership letters from Proud Ground and other CLT organizations.

---

## 4. Recommended Engagement with Proud Ground

Even under the independent formation path, Proud Ground is a valuable ally:

- **Technical assistance agreement** for CLT best practices, ground lease drafting review, and stewardship training
- **Letter of support** for grant applications and seller negotiations
- **Advisory role** — invite a Proud Ground representative to serve on MCLT's advisory board (not governing board, to preserve independence)
- **Future co-stewardship** if MCLT later develops affordable housing on the Property, Proud Ground could steward the residential component under a separate ground lease

---

## 5. Recommendation

**Form an independent Oregon nonprofit public benefit corporation and apply for 501(c)(3) status under the full Form 1023.** Engage Proud Ground in an advisory and technical assistance capacity only. This structure gives MCLT the legal flexibility, governance autonomy, and mission alignment required for a commercial/community CLT project of this scope.

Proceed immediately to drafting Articles of Incorporation (see companion document: `02-articles-of-incorporation-draft.md`).
