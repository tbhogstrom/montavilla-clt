### ARTICLE V — PURPOSE

**Section 5.1. General Purpose.** The Corporation is organized and shall be operated exclusively for charitable, educational, and community development purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the "Code"), and the corresponding provisions of any future United States internal revenue law.

**Section 5.2. Specific Purposes.** Without limiting the generality of Section 5.1, the specific purposes of the Corporation are:

(a) To acquire, hold, and steward real property in the Montavilla neighborhood of Portland, Oregon, and surrounding communities, removing such property from the speculative real estate market and holding it in perpetuity for community benefit;

(b) To operate as a community land trust, retaining ownership of land and entering into long-term ground leases with individuals, organizations, and enterprises that use the land for purposes consistent with the Corporation's mission;

(c) To preserve and protect community gathering spaces, farmers markets, public markets, and other commercial and community-use facilities that serve the social, economic, and cultural needs of the surrounding neighborhood;

(d) To support local food systems, small-scale agriculture, and food-related enterprises by providing access to affordable land, commercial kitchen facilities, cold storage, and market infrastructure;

(e) To develop, maintain, and steward green space, community gardens, and ecologically beneficial landscapes on land held by the Corporation;

(f) To lessen the burdens of government by providing community infrastructure and public gathering spaces that serve functions otherwise borne by municipal, county, or state government;

(g) To engage in community education regarding land stewardship, community ownership models, and the role of community land trusts in equitable development;

(h) To do any and all lawful acts and things that may be necessary, useful, suitable, or proper for the furtherance or accomplishment of, or in connection with, any of the foregoing purposes; provided, however, that all such activities shall be consistent with the Corporation's status as an organization exempt from federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code.

**Section 5.3. Limitations on Activities.** Notwithstanding any other provision of these Articles:

(a) The Corporation shall not carry on any activities not permitted to be carried on by a corporation exempt from federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, or by a corporation to which contributions are deductible under Section 170(c)(2) of the Code;

(b) No substantial part of the activities of the Corporation shall consist of carrying on propaganda, or otherwise attempting to influence legislation (except as otherwise permitted by Section 501(h) of the Code), and the Corporation shall not participate in, or intervene in (including the publishing or distributing of statements), any political campaign on behalf of (or in opposition to) any candidate for public office;

(c) The Corporation shall not be operated for the primary purpose of carrying on a trade or business for profit.

### ARTICLE VI — MEMBERSHIP

**Section 6.1. Members.** The Corporation shall have members. The qualifications, rights, and obligations of members shall be set forth in the Bylaws.

**Section 6.2. Classes of Membership.** The Bylaws shall establish the following classes of membership, consistent with the community land trust model:

(a) **Ground Lease Members** — Individuals and organizations holding active ground leases with the Corporation;

(b) **Community Members** — Individuals who reside or operate a business within the Corporation's service area, as defined in the Bylaws;

(c) **General Members** — All other individuals who support the mission of the Corporation and meet such qualifications as the Bylaws may prescribe.

---

### ARTICLE VII — BOARD OF DIRECTORS

**Section 7.1. Governance.** The affairs of the Corporation shall be managed by a Board of Directors.

**Section 7.2. Number and Composition.** The Board of Directors shall consist of not fewer than nine (9) and not more than fifteen (15) directors, as set forth in the Bylaws.

**Section 7.3. Tripartite Board Structure.** Consistent with the community land trust governance model, the Board of Directors shall be composed of three (3) equal constituencies:

(a) **One-third (1/3) Ground Lease Representatives** — Elected by and from the Ground Lease Members;

(b) **One-third (1/3) Community Representatives** — Elected by and from the Community Members, including residents, local business operators, and neighborhood organization representatives;

(c) **One-third (1/3) Public Interest Representatives** — Appointed or elected in accordance with the Bylaws, representing expertise in areas such as law, finance, real estate, urban planning, agriculture, or nonprofit management.

### ARTICLE IX — LAND STEWARDSHIP COVENANT

**Section 9.1. Perpetual Land Stewardship.** Any real property acquired by the Corporation shall be held in perpetuity for the benefit of the community. The Corporation shall not sell, transfer, or dispose of any interest in land except as follows:

(a) Ground leases of ninety-nine (99) years or longer for uses consistent with the Corporation's mission;

(b) Transfers to another community land trust or 501(c)(3) organization with a substantially similar mission, approved by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Board of Directors and a majority vote of the membership;

(c) Conservation easements, deed restrictions, or other encumbrances that further the Corporation's charitable purposes.

**Section 9.2. Anti-Speculation Provision.** No ground lease, sublease, or other transfer of an interest in Corporation-held land shall permit the lessee or transferee to realize speculative gain from the resale or transfer of such interest. All ground leases shall contain resale restrictions and formulas that preserve affordability and community benefit.

---

### ARTICLE X — DISSOLUTION

**Section 10.1.** Upon dissolution or final liquidation of the Corporation, after payment of all debts and liabilities, the remaining assets of the Corporation shall be distributed as follows:

(a) **Real property** (including all land, improvements, and interests therein) shall be transferred to one or more community land trusts organized and operated exclusively for charitable purposes and exempt from federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, which shall hold such property subject to restrictions substantially similar to those contained in these Articles and the Corporation's ground leases; or, if no such organization exists or is willing to accept the transfer,

(b) **Real property** shall be transferred to one or more organizations organized and operated exclusively for charitable, educational, or community development purposes and exempt from federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, or to the State of Oregon, or to a political subdivision thereof, for exclusively public purposes; and

(c) **All other assets** shall be distributed to one or more organizations organized and operated exclusively for purposes described in Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, or to the federal government, or to a state or local government, for exclusively public purposes.

**Section 10.2.** No part of the assets of the Corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, any director, officer, member, or private individual, except that the Corporation shall be authorized to pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and to make payments and distributions in furtherance of its exempt purposes.

*This dissolution provision satisfies the requirements of IRC 501(c)(3) and Treas. Reg. 1.501(c)(3)-1(b)(4). See also ORS 65.621–65.677 (dissolution of Oregon nonprofit corporations).*

### ARTICLE XI — INDEMNIFICATION

The Corporation shall indemnify its directors, officers, employees, and agents to the fullest extent permitted by ORS 65.387–65.414 and any amendments thereto.

---

### ARTICLE XII — AMENDMENT

These Articles of Incorporation may be amended in the manner prescribed by ORS 65.431–65.441. Any amendment that would alter the Corporation's charitable purposes, land stewardship covenant (Article IX), dissolution provisions (Article X), or tripartite board structure (Article VII, Section 7.3) shall require approval by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Board of Directors and a majority vote of the membership.

---

### ARTICLE XIII — NONDISCRIMINATION

The Corporation shall not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age, disability, or any other characteristic protected by federal, state, or local law.
