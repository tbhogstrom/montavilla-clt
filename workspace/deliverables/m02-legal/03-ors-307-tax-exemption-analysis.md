## 1. Executive Summary

The Montavilla Community Land Trust intends to acquire the 0.84-acre parcel at 7700 SE Stark Street, Portland, Oregon (Parcel R198852), and hold it in perpetuity as a community land trust. This analysis evaluates the available Oregon property tax exemption statutes. **The primary finding is that ORS 307.540–307.548 — often cited as the "CLT tax exemption" — does not apply to this project.** That statute is limited to low-income housing. However, **ORS 307.130 (charitable institution exemption) offers a viable path**, subject to conditions analyzed below.

---

## 2. ORS 307.540–307.548: Low-Income Housing Exemption

### 2.1 What These Statutes Actually Cover

Despite frequent association with community land trusts, ORS 307.540–307.548 is a **nonprofit low-income housing exemption**, not a CLT-specific exemption. The phrase "community land trust" does not appear in these sections.

### 2.2 Eligibility Requirements (ORS 307.540, 307.541)

To qualify, the property owner must be:

- A 501(c)(3) or 501(c)(4) corporation exempt under IRC 501(a)
- Property must be **"occupied by low-income persons"** as housing, or held for development of low-income housing
- Property must be "actually and exclusively used" for the organization's exempt purpose
- Governing body (City of Portland) must approve the exemption per ORS 307.547

### 2.3 Income Limits (ORS 307.540(2))

The governing body selects from three income tiers:
- (a) 60% Area Median Income (AMI) flat
- (b) 60% AMI initial year / 80% AMI subsequent years
- (c) 80% AMI with 60% average for LIHTC properties

### 2.4 Application to 7700 SE Stark Street

**This exemption DOES NOT APPLY.** Risk rating: **N/A — statute is inapplicable.**

| Requirement | MCLT Project | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Property used for low-income housing | Farmers market, commissary kitchen, community hub | **FAIL** |
| Occupied by low-income persons | Commercial/community use, not residential occupancy | **FAIL** |
| Portland's NPLTE program implementation | Explicitly excludes commercial and retail spaces | **FAIL** |

**Conclusion:** ORS 307.540–307.548 cannot provide property tax relief for this project in its current form. This statute would only become relevant if MCLT later develops affordable housing on the Property.

### 2.5 Sunset Provision

Note: ORS 307.540–307.548 **sunsets July 1, 2027**. Even for qualifying housing projects, the Oregon Legislature must reauthorize the program before that date.

## 3. ORS 307.130: Charitable Institution Exemption — PRIMARY PATH

### 3.1 Statute Overview

ORS 307.130 provides property tax exemption for property owned or being purchased by qualifying institutions that is "actually and exclusively occupied or used in the literary, benevolent, charitable or scientific work carried on by such institutions."

### 3.2 Qualifying Institutions

The property owner must be:
- A literary, benevolent, charitable, or scientific institution (ORS 307.130(2)(a))
- An organization described in IRC 501(c)(3) is generally presumed charitable

### 3.3 Key Legal Tests

Oregon courts have developed a multi-factor test for ORS 307.130 eligibility. The leading case is *SW Oregon Publishing Co. v. Dept. of Revenue*, 10 OTR 324 (1987), which established:

1. **Charitable Purpose Test** — Is the organization's primary purpose charitable?
2. **Exclusive Use Test** — Is the property "actually and exclusively" used for the charitable purpose?
3. **Public Benefit Test** — Does the use primarily benefit the public rather than private individuals?
4. **No Private Inurement Test** — Do net earnings benefit any private individual?

### 3.4 Application to MCLT / 7700 SE Stark Street

| Factor | Analysis | Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| **Charitable Purpose** | MCLT's exempt purpose includes community development, preserving public gathering spaces, supporting local food systems, and lessening the burdens of government. These are recognized charitable purposes under IRC 501(c)(3). | **STRONG** |
| **Exclusive Use** | The entire parcel will be used for the farmers market, community hub, commissary kitchen, green space, and related community programming. The key risk is **commercial tenants** (e.g., Portland Guitar Repair) — any portion leased to for-profit tenants at market rent may be excluded from exemption. | **MODERATE — requires structuring** |
| **Public Benefit** | A community farmers market and public gathering space primarily benefit the general public, not specific private individuals. | **STRONG** |
| **No Private Inurement** | MCLT's Articles prohibit private inurement. However, below-market ground leases to commercial tenants must be structured to avoid the appearance of private benefit. | **MODERATE — requires structuring** |

### 3.5 Structuring for ORS 307.130 Compliance

To maximize the likelihood of property tax exemption, MCLT should:

1. **Dedicate the entire Property to charitable use.** The farmers market, community hub, commissary kitchen, and green space are all charitable uses. Community programming, educational events, and public access should be documented and emphasized.

2. **Structure commercial tenancies carefully.** Any for-profit tenant (e.g., Portland Guitar Repair) should operate under a ground lease or sublease at below-market rates, with use restrictions aligned to MCLT's charitable mission. Alternatively, the portion of the Property occupied by for-profit tenants may need to be excluded from the exemption application (partial exemption under ORS 307.130(3)).

3. **Document public benefit extensively.** Maintain records of: number of market vendors, visitors, community events, educational programs, and charitable activities occurring on the Property.

4. **Apply separately for each tax year.** ORS 307.130 exemptions are not permanent — they must be maintained through continued qualifying use. Annual filing with the Multnomah County Assessor is required by March 1 of the assessment year.

### 3.6 Risk Assessment

| Risk | Level | Consequence | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| County Assessor denies exemption due to commercial activity on site | **MEDIUM** | Full property tax liability (~$55,000–$75,000/year at current assessed value) | Structure all uses as charitable; limit for-profit subletting; seek advisory opinion from Assessor pre-acquisition |
| Partial exemption granted (community areas exempt, commercial tenant areas not) | **MEDIUM** | Partial tax liability; manageable but reduces operating margins | Budget for partial tax liability in financial projections; structure leases to pass through proportional tax costs |
| Exemption granted but challenged in future years | **LOW** | Potential back-tax liability | Maintain meticulous use documentation; file timely annual applications |

## 4. Other Potential Exemptions

### 4.1 ORS 307.110 — Property of the State, Counties, and Political Subdivisions

If MCLT enters into an intergovernmental agreement where the City of Portland or Multnomah County holds a partial interest in the Property (e.g., a conservation easement, deed restriction, or co-ownership), the governmental interest may be exempt under ORS 307.110. This is a secondary strategy worth exploring.

### 4.2 ORS 307.475 — Open Space Land

Portions of the Property dedicated to green space, community gardens, or ecological preservation may qualify for special assessment as "open space land" under ORS 307.475–307.495. This would reduce (not eliminate) the tax burden on those portions.

### 4.3 ORS 307.455 — Farm Use Special Assessment

If any portion of the Property is used for agricultural purposes (community garden, demonstration farm, vendor growing space), it may qualify for farm-use special assessment. However, the parcel's urban zoning (CM2 or similar) and small size (0.84 acres) may preclude this.

### 4.4 City of Portland Community Benefit Agreement

Portland has discretion to negotiate property tax abatement or exemption through a Community Benefit Agreement ("CBA") in connection with publicly supported projects. If MCLT secures CDBG funding or other City support, a CBA could include tax relief provisions.

## 6. Recommended Actions

1. **Do not rely on ORS 307.540–307.548.** It does not apply to this project.

2. **Structure the Property for ORS 307.130 eligibility** from day one. All uses should be documented as charitable, educational, or community development activities.

3. **Engage Multnomah County Assessor's office** before acquisition to seek an informal advisory opinion on likely exemption treatment.

4. **File the ORS 307.130 exemption application** by March 1 of the first assessment year following acquisition.

5. **Explore ORS 307.475 open space assessment** for the green space and landscape portions of the Property.

6. **Include property tax projections** in all financial models and grant applications, using the partial-exemption base case.

7. **Consult Oregon property tax counsel** for a formal opinion before closing. The exemption analysis may affect the acquisition structure and financing terms.
