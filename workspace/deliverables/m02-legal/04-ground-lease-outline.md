# Ground Lease Outline & Key Terms

## Montavilla Community Land Trust — 99-Year Community Ground Lease

**Prepared by:** Statton, Legal Counsel
**Date:** April 13, 2026
**Re:** Ground Lease Framework for 7700 SE Stark Street, Portland, Oregon

---

## 1. Overview

The community land trust model separates ownership of **land** (held by the CLT in perpetuity) from ownership of **improvements** (held by the lessee). The ground lease is the legal instrument that governs this relationship. This document outlines the key terms and structure for MCLT's ground leases at 7700 SE Stark Street.

MCLT's ground lease will differ from a typical residential CLT ground lease (such as Proud Ground's homeownership model) because the lessees will be **commercial and community-use tenants** — principally the Montavilla Farmers Market, a commissary kitchen/community hub operator, and potentially legacy commercial tenants such as Portland Guitar Repair.

| Party | Role |

## 3. Recitals

The ground lease shall include recitals establishing:

1. MCLT's charitable mission and community land trust purpose
2. The Property's significance to the Montavilla neighborhood
3. The community process by which the Property was acquired
4. The intent to remove the Property permanently from the speculative real estate market
5. The lessee's understanding of and commitment to the CLT model
6. Applicable authority: Oregon property law, ORS Chapter 93 (conveyancing), ORS Chapter 105 (property rights)

## 4. Key Terms

### 4.1 Term of Lease

| Provision | Term |
|---|---|
| **Initial Term** | 99 years from the Commencement Date |
| **Renewal** | Automatic renewal for successive 99-year terms, unless terminated for cause |
| **Early Termination** | Only for material default, after notice and cure period (see Section 4.11) |

**Legal note:** Oregon does not impose a statutory maximum on ground lease terms. A 99-year term is standard in CLT practice and is long enough to support conventional and CDFI financing. Per ORS 93.220, a leasehold interest exceeding one year must be in writing.

### 4.2 Premises

- **Land only.** MCLT leases the ground; the Lessee owns any improvements constructed or placed on the leased premises.
- **Legal description:** [Lot/parcel description, Parcel R198852, 7700 SE Stark Street, Portland, OR]
- **Survey and site plan** attached as Exhibit A, delineating the leased premises if less than the full parcel.

### 4.3 Permitted Uses

The Lessee shall use the leased premises exclusively for one or more of the following purposes:

(a) Operation of a public farmers market (year-round or seasonal)
(b) Community gathering space and public events
(c) Commissary kitchen, commercial kitchen, and food processing facilities
(d) Commercial cold storage and deep freezer facilities for market vendors
(e) Retail and commercial uses that directly support the farmers market and local food economy
(f) Community education, workshops, and programming
(g) Green space, community gardens, and ecological stewardship
(h) Such other uses as MCLT may approve in writing, consistent with its charitable mission

**Prohibited uses:** The Lessee shall not use the premises for any of the following without prior written consent of MCLT:
- Residential occupancy (except caretaker quarters, if approved)
- Uses that conflict with MCLT's 501(c)(3) exempt status
- Activities that would cause the Property to lose its property tax exemption
- Any use that generates excessive noise, environmental contamination, or nuisance
- Cannabis dispensary or production (to preserve federal funding eligibility)

### 4.4 Ground Lease Fee

| Component | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Base Ground Lease Fee** | $[AMOUNT]/month | Set at below-market rate to reflect MCLT's charitable mission. Initial rate based on operational sustainability, not market rent. |
| **Annual Adjustment** | CPI-U (Portland-Salem) or 2%, whichever is less | Prevents lease fee from outpacing lessee's revenue capacity |
| **Community Benefit Offset** | Up to [X]% reduction | Available to lessees providing documented community programming, free/reduced-cost vendor stalls, or charitable services |

**Structuring note:** The ground lease fee should be set to cover MCLT's annual stewardship costs (insurance, property taxes if any, administrative overhead, reserve fund contributions) without generating profit. This supports both 501(c)(3) compliance and ORS 307.130 exemption eligibility.

### 4.5 Ownership of Improvements

| Principle | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Lessee owns improvements** | All structures, fixtures, and improvements constructed by the Lessee on the leased premises shall be the property of the Lessee during the term of the lease |
| **Construction approval** | All new construction and material alterations require prior written approval of MCLT, which shall not be unreasonably withheld |
| **Design standards** | MCLT may establish design guidelines consistent with its mission (sustainability, accessibility, community character) |
| **End of term** | Upon expiration or termination, improvements transfer to MCLT at fair market value (depreciated), or Lessee may remove non-permanent improvements at Lessee's expense |
| **Insurance** | Lessee shall maintain property insurance on improvements; MCLT shall maintain property insurance on the land |

### 4.6 Transfer and Assignment Restrictions

This is the core anti-speculation mechanism of the CLT model.

**Section 4.6.1. No Transfer Without Consent.** The Lessee shall not assign, sublease, or transfer the ground lease or any interest therein without the prior written consent of MCLT, which consent may be withheld in MCLT's sole discretion.

**Section 4.6.2. Right of First Refusal.** If the Lessee desires to sell, assign, or transfer its leasehold interest or improvements:

(a) Lessee shall provide MCLT with written notice of the proposed transfer, including all material terms;
(b) MCLT shall have sixty (60) days to exercise a right of first refusal to purchase the improvements and/or assume the leasehold interest at the Resale Price (Section 4.6.3);
(c) If MCLT does not exercise its right, the Lessee may transfer to a third party, subject to MCLT's approval of the transferee and the transferee's assumption of all ground lease obligations.

**Section 4.6.3. Resale Formula.** If the Lessee sells its improvements or leasehold interest, the maximum Resale Price shall be calculated as follows:

> **Resale Price = Original Purchase Price + Documented Capital Improvements (depreciated) + [X]% of Market Appreciation**

The specific appreciation share percentage shall be set in each ground lease based on the type of use and community benefit provided. The formula shall be designed to:
- Allow the Lessee to recover its investment and a reasonable return
- Prevent speculative windfall
- Preserve affordability for the next lessee

**Section 4.6.4. Approved Transferees.** Any transferee must:
(a) Demonstrate capacity to operate the premises consistent with the permitted uses;
(b) Agree to assume all ground lease obligations;
(c) Be approved by MCLT's Board of Directors;
(d) Execute a new ground lease or an assignment and assumption agreement.

### 4.7 Maintenance and Stewardship

- **Lessee responsibility:** Day-to-day maintenance of improvements, landscaping within the leased premises, utilities, and operational costs
- **MCLT responsibility:** Major land stewardship (environmental remediation if needed, boundary maintenance), property tax, and land insurance
- **Shared responsibility:** Capital improvements to shared infrastructure (parking, paths, utilities) — cost-sharing formula to be established
- **Annual inspection:** MCLT shall have the right to conduct an annual inspection of the premises, with reasonable notice, to verify compliance with the ground lease and stewardship standards

### 4.8 Environmental Provisions

(a) Lessee shall comply with all applicable environmental laws, including Oregon DEQ requirements
(b) Lessee shall not use, store, or dispose of hazardous materials on the premises except as incidental to permitted uses (e.g., commercial kitchen cleaning supplies) and in compliance with applicable law
(c) **Pre-acquisition Phase I ESA recommended** — MCLT should commission a Phase I Environmental Site Assessment before closing. The Property's history as a commercial site may present environmental considerations.
(d) MCLT shall retain responsibility for any pre-existing contamination. Lessee shall be responsible for contamination caused by Lessee's activities.

### 4.9 Insurance Requirements

| Coverage | Responsible Party | Minimum |
|---|---|---|
| General liability | Lessee | $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 aggregate |
| Property insurance (improvements) | Lessee | Replacement cost |
| Property insurance (land) | MCLT | As required by lender |
| Workers' compensation | Lessee | As required by Oregon law |
| Directors & Officers | MCLT | $1,000,000 |
| Umbrella/excess | Both | As determined by risk assessment |

MCLT shall be named as additional insured on all Lessee policies.

### 4.10 Financing Provisions

To enable the Lessee to obtain financing for improvements:

(a) **Leasehold mortgage permitted** — Lessee may mortgage its leasehold interest and improvements with MCLT's prior written consent
(b) **Lender protections** — The ground lease shall include standard "mortgagee protection" provisions: notice to lender of default, lender's right to cure, and lender's right to a new lease upon termination
(c) **MCLT subordination** — MCLT shall not subordinate its fee interest in the land to any leasehold mortgage. The lender's security interest is limited to the leasehold and improvements.
(d) **Estoppel certificates** — MCLT shall provide estoppel certificates to Lessee's lenders upon reasonable request

### 4.11 Default and Remedies

**Section 4.11.1. Events of Default by Lessee:**
(a) Failure to pay ground lease fee for sixty (60) days after written notice
(b) Use of premises for purposes not permitted under Section 4.3
(c) Unauthorized transfer or assignment
(d) Material violation of environmental provisions
(e) Abandonment of the premises for more than twelve (12) consecutive months
(f) Failure to maintain required insurance

**Section 4.11.2. Cure Period.**
(a) Monetary default: 60 days from written notice
(b) Non-monetary default: 120 days from written notice, or such longer period as may be reasonably necessary to cure, provided Lessee is diligently pursuing cure

**Section 4.11.3. Remedies.**
(a) If default is not cured, MCLT may terminate the ground lease upon 30 days' additional written notice
(b) Upon termination, MCLT shall purchase the Lessee's improvements at fair market value (depreciated), less any amounts owed to MCLT
(c) MCLT shall have all remedies available at law or in equity, including specific performance

### 4.12 Dispute Resolution

(a) **Mediation first.** Any dispute arising under the ground lease shall first be submitted to mediation through a mutually agreed mediator or, if the parties cannot agree, through the Multnomah County Circuit Court mediation program.
(b) **Arbitration.** If mediation is unsuccessful within 60 days, the dispute shall be submitted to binding arbitration under the rules of the American Arbitration Association, conducted in Portland, Oregon.
(c) **Governing law.** The ground lease shall be governed by the laws of the State of Oregon.

### 4.13 Recording

The ground lease, or a memorandum thereof, shall be recorded in the deed records of Multnomah County, Oregon, to provide constructive notice to all parties. Per ORS 93.710, a memorandum of lease is sufficient for recording purposes.

## 5. Ground Lease Structure Options

MCLT has three structural options for the ground lease architecture at 7700 SE Stark:

### Option A: Single Master Lessee

```
MCLT (fee owner)
  └── 99-year Ground Lease → Montavilla Farmers Market (Master Lessee)
        ├── Sublease → Commissary Kitchen Operator
        ├── Sublease → Portland Guitar Repair
        └── Sublease → Other Community Tenants
```

**Pros:** Simple. One primary relationship for MCLT to manage. Market organization controls day-to-day tenant mix.
**Cons:** Concentration risk — if the Market organization fails, MCLT must restructure all sublease relationships. Market organization must have capacity to be a landlord.

### Option B: Multiple Direct Ground Leases

```
MCLT (fee owner)
  ├── 99-year Ground Lease → Montavilla Farmers Market (market area)
  ├── 99-year Ground Lease → Commissary Kitchen Operator (building)
  ├── 99-year Ground Lease → Portland Guitar Repair (commercial space)
  └── 99-year Ground Lease → Community Programming Organization (event space)
```

**Pros:** Diversified risk. Each tenant has a direct relationship with MCLT. Failure of one tenant doesn't cascade.
**Cons:** More complex management. MCLT needs operational capacity to manage multiple leases. Potential conflicts over shared infrastructure.

### Option C: Master Lease to MCLT Operating Subsidiary (Recommended)

```
MCLT (fee owner)
  └── 99-year Ground Lease → MCLT Operations LLC (single-member LLC, MCLT as sole member)
        ├── Sublease → Montavilla Farmers Market
        ├── Sublease → Commissary Kitchen Operator
        ├── Sublease → Portland Guitar Repair
        └── Managed directly → Green space, community programming
```

**Pros:** Liability separation between land stewardship (MCLT) and operations (LLC). MCLT retains full control. Single-member LLC is a disregarded entity for tax purposes — no additional tax filing. Operationally flexible.
**Cons:** Additional entity formation ($100 Oregon LLC filing fee). Slightly more complex corporate structure.

**Recommendation:** Option C provides the best balance of liability protection, operational flexibility, and MCLT control. However, Option A is the simplest path if the Montavilla Farmers Market organization has the capacity and willingness to serve as master lessee. This decision should be made in consultation with the Market's leadership.

## 7. Critical Legal Considerations

### 7.1 Bargain Sale Structuring

If the Property is acquired below the $4,999,000 listing price via a bargain sale (where the seller accepts below-market price and claims a charitable deduction for the difference), the ground lease structure must be in place before closing to demonstrate that:
- MCLT is a qualified 501(c)(3) organization
- The Property will be used exclusively for charitable purposes
- The seller's charitable deduction is supported by a qualified appraisal (IRC 170(f)(11))

### 7.2 Lender Requirements

CDFI and conventional lenders financing the acquisition will require review and approval of the ground lease. Standard lender requirements include:
- Minimum remaining lease term (typically 30+ years beyond loan maturity)
- Mortgagee protection clauses (notice, cure rights, new lease rights)
- Estoppel certificate provisions
- No subordination of the fee interest

### 7.3 Property Tax Exemption Coordination

The ground lease structure must be coordinated with the ORS 307.130 property tax exemption strategy (see companion document: `03-ors-307-tax-exemption-analysis.md`). Key considerations:
- Lease terms to for-profit tenants must be structured at below-market rates to support the "charitable use" characterization
- The ground lease should explicitly require tenants to cooperate with MCLT's tax exemption applications
- Any sublease income must be below MCLT's cost of stewardship to avoid characterization as "unrelated business income" under IRC 512

### 7.4 Oregon Recording Requirements

Per ORS 93.710 and ORS 93.260, the ground lease (or memorandum of lease) must be:
- In writing
- Signed by the grantor (MCLT)
- Acknowledged (notarized)
- Recorded in Multnomah County deed records
- Accompanied by recording fee (currently $86 first page, $5 each additional page)

## Limitations

This document is an outline and summary of key terms for a community land trust ground lease. It does not constitute a complete ground lease and is not intended for execution. A full ground lease should be drafted by licensed Oregon real estate counsel, reviewed by all parties, and negotiated before execution. All statutory citations should be verified for current applicability. The financial terms (ground lease fees, resale formulas, insurance minimums) are placeholders and must be determined based on the specific circumstances of the acquisition and lessee negotiations.
