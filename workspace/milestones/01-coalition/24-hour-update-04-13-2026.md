# 24-Hour Update — April 13, 2026

**Subject:** You were one of 7. Here's where we stand.

Hey —

You signed the Montavilla Community Land Trust petition yesterday. You were one of seven people. I want to be honest with you about where things are after 24 hours.

**My name is Tyler Falcon.** I'm a Montavilla neighbor and I'm the one who started this. Not an organization, not a nonprofit — just someone who saw the "for sale" sign go up on 7700 SE Stark and thought: we should have a say in this.

**What happened yesterday:**

We launched Sunday and posted to Reddit. The thread got 94 upvotes and 78 comments before it was taken down. The feedback was honest — some people love the idea, a lot think $5M is absurd, many think the lot should be housing, others say the market could move somewhere cheaper.

They're not wrong to ask those questions. I'm asking them too.

**What we're actually trying to do:**

This isn't about locking in one plan. It's about whether Montavilla gets a voice in what happens to that lot — or whether the decision gets made by a real estate investor in Michigan and whoever writes the biggest check.

A community land trust means the community decides. Maybe that's a year-round market commons. Maybe it's affordable housing with market space. Maybe it's something none of us have thought of yet.
