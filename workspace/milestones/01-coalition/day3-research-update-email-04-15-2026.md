### 1. What Is a Community Land Trust?
**montavillalandtrust.org/what-is-a-clt**

If you've been telling friends about this and struggling to explain what a CLT actually is — this is the page to send them.

It covers the core mechanics: a CLT buys land and holds it permanently. The land never goes back on the speculative market. Buildings and improvements on top of the land can be owned by tenants, businesses, or housing residents — but the ground stays in community hands, governed by a board that represents the people who live and work there.

The page also breaks down the three kinds of land CLTs protect — housing, agriculture, and commercial — and profiles four CLTs that started in situations closest to ours: Athens (farmers market), Rondo (commercial district), Sawmill (single property), and Lopez Island (mixed-use rural).

The lesson that keeps coming up: **the difference between a community institution that lasts and one that doesn't is who owns the dirt underneath it.**

---

### 2. CLT Positioning Map
**montavillalandtrust.org/clt-map**

This is a visualization of 30+ U.S. community land trusts plotted across three axes: housing, agriculture, and commercial.

Most CLTs cluster heavily toward housing — that's where the federal funding is, and it's where the movement started. A smaller number protect agricultural land. An even smaller number hold commercial or community-use properties.

What Montavilla is proposing sits at a rare intersection: commercial (the market and local businesses) and agricultural (the vendors, the food system). There are very few CLTs in the country positioned where we'd be. That's both the opportunity and the challenge — fewer models to copy, but also a genuine gap in what CLTs are doing nationally.

The map is interactive. Hover over any dot to see the CLT, where it is, what it holds, and how it's funded. It's worth five minutes of your time.

### 3. U.S. CLT Meta Analysis
**montavillalandtrust.org/research**

This is the deep read. A full research page covering:

- **The national landscape:** 308 CLTs across 48 states. The movement has nearly doubled since 2006.
- **CLT typology:** How different CLTs are structured, what they hold, how they're governed.
- **National inventory:** A directory of CLTs organized by type and geography.
- **Funding pathways:** How CLTs actually get funded — CDBG, CDFIs, state housing trust funds, community bonds, philanthropic capital, bargain sales.
- **Closest analogs to MCLT:** Six case studies of CLTs that started where we're starting — Rondo CLT in St. Paul (commercial district saved by a city land transfer), Athens Land Trust (farmers market), Sawmill CLT in Albuquerque (founded around a single property), Partnership in Property in Minneapolis (commercial CLT incubated inside a housing CLT).
- **Lessons learned:** What works, what doesn't, and what we should be paying attention to.

The single most important finding: **pre-positioning capital before a specific property is identified is essential.** Urban Land Conservancy in Denver put it plainly — "if capital isn't raised quickly enough, the land becomes too expensive." That's the clock we're racing.
