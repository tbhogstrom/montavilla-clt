### HOW THIS WORKS

This petition travels neighbor to neighbor, door to door.

Each block or street has a **Street Captain** — a neighbor who takes responsibility for circulating the petition on their street, collecting completed signature sheets, and returning them to MCLT. If someone handed you this sheet, they live near you and they think this matters.

If you want to become a Street Captain for your block, email **info@montavillalandtrust.org** or visit **montavillalandtrust.org**.

---

### WHO WE ARE

We're your neighbors. The Montavilla Community Land Trust is a volunteer-led effort started by residents of the 97215 zip code who want the neighborhood — not a developer — to decide the future of 7700 SE Stark Street.

We're not a company. We're not funded by the city. We're people who showed up at the farmers market and decided to do something about it.

---

### WHY THIS MATTERS

**The Montavilla Farmers Market's home is for sale.**

Since 2007, the market has operated on a 0.84-acre parcel at 7700 SE Stark Street — growing from 17 vendors to more than 70. In late 2024, the property owners notified the market they intend to sell. The property is now listed at **$4,999,000**.

If a developer closes, the market could be displaced permanently. The zoning allows up to four stories of commercial and residential development. There is no timeline guarantee. A sale could happen at any time.

**A Community Land Trust is how communities protect land permanently.**

A CLT acquires land and removes it from the speculative market — forever. The land is held in trust for the community. Buildings on top can be owned, leased, or operated by tenants — but the ground stays in community hands, governed by a board of the people who live and work here.

This model has been used in 308 CLTs across 48 states to protect farmers markets, commercial districts, housing, and neighborhood institutions. It works. The question is whether we can make it work here — and fast enough.

**The petition tells the city, sellers, and funders that Montavilla is organized and paying attention.**

Every signature is a neighbor saying: this neighborhood has a stake in what happens at 7700 SE Stark, and we want a seat at the table.

### PETITION STATEMENT

We, the undersigned residents and community members of Montavilla and the surrounding Portland neighborhoods, call on the City of Portland, Multnomah County, and all relevant stakeholders to:

1. **Recognize the community interest** in the 0.84-acre parcel at 7700 SE Stark Street — home of the Montavilla Farmers Market since 2007 — and treat any transfer of ownership as a matter of public concern.

2. **Support community-led acquisition efforts**, including but not limited to the formation of a Community Land Trust, that would permanently protect this site for neighborhood use and remove it from speculative development.

3. **Engage transparently** with neighborhood residents, market vendors, and community organizations throughout any sale or development process.

We believe that communities should have a meaningful role in the future of the places that define them. We are not opposed to change — we are asking for a voice in shaping it.
