# Montavilla Farmers Market's 20 Years in Portland

This is a big year for the Montavilla Farmers Market: They are entering their 20th year of operating one of the most beloved farmers markets on the east side of Portland. I sat down with Lisa Hebert one morning at Bipartisan Cafe to chat about the market and get a feel for the success of these past 20 years.

## Montavilla Farmers Market Founders

I was curious to know more about the people who started the Montavilla Farmers Market. Turns out it was a group of volunteers who worked with the Montavilla East Tabor Business Association (METBA) to found the market. Their website describes them as "a grassroots collection of neighbors who were inspired by visions of a fresh, local, food market in a shared community space within the growing SE Stark Street business district."

Their first market was Sunday, July 22nd, 2007. "The first year it was run just by volunteers," Lisa confirmed. "And then the next year they were able to pay their market manager a small stipend."

It made me think of the Rocky Butte Farmers Market, which also was founded by volunteers and has progressed to a place where the market manager has been designated a paid position.

## Lisa Hebert, Executive Director of the Montavilla Farmers Market

Lisa is the market's executive director, where she tackles much of the behind-the-scenes work — things like budgeting, financials, strategy, overseeing a handful of paid staff, marketing, and fundraising. She also works with their board — Montavilla Farmers Market is a **501(c)(6) nonprofit**.

She has lived in Montavilla since 2020, and worked for the Portland Farmers Market starting in 2016 before she moved to the neighborhood.

## Lisa's Early Experience With Farmers Markets

Lisa attended UC Santa Cruz, got involved with the sustainability center, taught a gardening class on campus, worked at the seed library, helped create a pop-up farmers market on campus, and interned at the Santa Cruz farmers market her senior year. She managed the Santa Cruz market the summer after graduation. Moved to Portland, cooked at a preschool, then was hired as a market manager by the Portland Farmers Market.

## Why a Market in Montavilla? Food Access.

"From looking at [the market's] documents and records, I think it was really important for having that food access in the neighborhood."

The market has accepted EBT benefits since early on. In 2010 the market began fundraising to match EBT benefits on its own; currently EBT matching runs through Farmers Market Fund (Oregon-wide org).

## The Current Location of the Montavilla Farmers Market

The current location at 7700 SE Stark has been their home since the start. The lot previously had a building belonging to **Beets Auto Body**, razed in 2020 after the property was sold in 2019. The lot was at one point an artist co-op (referenced food carts and artists). 2020's open lot configuration helped with COVID social distancing — "we were able to expand during 2020, which was great timing."

## A Potential Move?

> "There has been discussion online about the potential need for the Montavilla Farmers Market to move. **Please note: They are not moving right now, and nobody has asked them to move. But honestly, nobody knows what the future holds right now.**"

MFM has put out a survey to the community on this topic and is currently reviewing the responses.

## What Makes Montavilla Special

> "I think Montavilla is special for a couple of reasons. One of my favorite reasons is that I believe a lot of the participants at our market tend to be smaller farms and we tend to get the owner of the farm at the market. I think that's special, being able to actually be face-to-face with the person who's hands are in the dirt growing the food."

> "I also think that we're not a tourist market… we don't have a lot of tourism at our market specifically." (Compared to PSU and Hollywood, which are destination markets.)

> "Also, I think it's kind of special that we see the same people every week."

> "A recent survey revealed that **53% of people walked to the market**."

> "It's a medium large market, so ideally, someone can go and do most of their grocery shopping there. We try to curate the market to have a lot of staples for people."

## Market Growth

- **17 vendors** on opening day (2007)
- **~100 vendors** throughout the year now
- **~60 vendors** at any given typical summer market

> "The fact that the neighborhood is able to support all of those vendors is really amazing. And we hear from vendors that say Montavilla is where they're able to sustain their business — it's a really important market for them. For some of them, it's their best market that they're able to make their most amount of profit from."

> "This market is incredibly important — not only for the community, but those vendors who really rely on the support from our shoppers and from our organization."

## Yearly Anticipation

Lisa's spring marker: snap peas. "Spring is here! Food is coming!" Also garlic scapes.

## The Next 20 Years

> "I think farmers markets are hugely important to local agriculture and farmers. I've had a farmer tell me who does wholesale, 'We would not be here if it wasn't for the money that we're able to get at farmers markets because it's that direct-to-consumer purchase.' I think the farmers need farmers markets, and people crave that seasonal food. And yes, I think we'll be here because I think farmers markets just make sense."

## Final Thoughts (Lisa)

> "This neighborhood has been very supportive of the market through the years. It has been really amazing and has taken ownership of the market, which I think is really special. You can tell through the survey results that people really care about the market, really love the market, really want to see the best for it."

> "I was a little nervous that we'd get maybe some negative comments, but it was so positive! And so many people were saying, 'Thank you for the work with this.' There's a lot of support. And in the past when we've done fundraising, we've always had a lot of support from the community. I think that's really special."

> "And as someone who also lives in their community, it really feels like Montavilla is kind of its own bubble a little bit. We're almost at our own town. We have these amazing businesses — you don't have to leave if you don't want to!"
