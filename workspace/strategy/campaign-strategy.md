# Montavilla CLT — Campaign Strategy

**Date:** 2026-04-13
**Author:** Maven, Campaign Director

---

## Situation Assessment

We have a live campaign website, a published prospectus, and a Market Day event behind us (April 12). The digital infrastructure is solid — petition system, pledge ledger, email confirmations, print flyer generator, all deployed on Vercel with Neon database.

What we don't have yet: a legal entity, retained counsel, formalized partnerships, or a capital stack that pencils.

The property is listed at $4,999,000. That's the headline number we need to solve.

## What's Working

1. **The Website** — Professional, urgent, clear call-to-action hierarchy (sign → pledge → join → share). The urgency bar and live signature counter create social proof momentum.

2. **The Prospectus** — Provides credibility with serious stakeholders (funders, Proud Ground, city officials).

3. **The Vision** — Four-part plan (CLT + Year-Round Pavilion + Mass Timber Hub + Green Space) is compelling and differentiates from "just save the market."

4. **Digital-to-Ground Pipeline** — Petition captures skills ("how can you help"), enabling fast steering committee assembly.

## What Needs Work

### 1. Speed to Entity
We cannot accept grants, sign contracts, or receive tax-deductible gifts without legal standing. Two paths:
- **Fast:** Fiscal sponsorship through an existing 501(c)(3) (Proud Ground? Social Venture Partners? Montavilla Neighborhood Association?)
- **Slower but durable:** File Oregon Articles of Incorporation + 501(c)(3) application (4-12 months for IRS determination)

**Recommendation:** Do BOTH. Fiscal sponsor now, file for independent status in parallel.

### 2. Seller Engagement
We have zero relationship with the property seller. The listing is public at $4,999,000. We need:
- Identify the listing broker
- Send a Letter of Interest (not a formal offer — a signal of serious community intent)
- Explore option contract (pay for 6-12 months of exclusivity while we assemble financing)
- Understand seller motivation (timeline? open to bargain sale? tax benefits of donation/below-market?)

### 3. Capital Stack Architecture
$4,999,000 won't come from one source. Likely structure:

| Source | Amount | Timeline |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Community pledges/crowdfund | $200K-$500K | Month 2-8 |
| Anchor donors | $250K-$750K | Month 2-6 |
| CDFI bridge loan | $1M-$2M | Month 4-8 |
| Grants (USDA, Meyer, CDBG, etc.) | $1M-$2M | Month 6-14 |
| Bargain sale discount | $500K-$1M | Negotiation |
| Community bonds | $500K-$1M | Month 6-12 |

Total potential: $3.5M-$7.25M (range includes negotiated price reduction)

### 4. Proud Ground Clarification
The roadmap references "partnering with Proud Ground" but the About section disclaims we're independent. Need to resolve this ambiguity with a real conversation. Options:
- **Advisory only** — they consult, we build independently
- **Affiliate/chapter** — we operate under their umbrella
- **Co-stewardship** — joint ground lease administration
- **Technical assistance** — formal TA agreement with scope

### 5. Coalition Breadth
Current petition captures individuals. We need institutional allies:
- Montavilla Farmers Market (organizational support, not just vendors)
- Montavilla Neighborhood Association
- Portland Guitar Repair (co-tenant — their fate is tied to ours)
- Local businesses on SE Stark corridor
- City Commissioner offices (especially those with housing/land use portfolios)
- Metro (regional land use authority)

## Campaign Phases

### Phase 1: Proof of Demand (Now — Week 4)
- Publish petition/pledge numbers from Market Day
- Reach 500 petition signatures
- Reach $100K in pledges
- Hold first steering committee meeting
- Media coverage (1-2 stories)
- Fiscal sponsor secured

### Phase 2: Legal Foundation (Week 2 — Month 3)
- Incorporate nonprofit
- Retain attorney
- Formalize Proud Ground relationship
- File 501(c)(3)
- Establish governance structure

### Phase 3: Capital Assembly (Month 2 — Month 10)
- Launch public crowdfund
- Anchor donor asks
- Submit first grant applications
- CDFI pre-development conversations
- Community bond legal review

### Phase 4: Deal Execution (Month 6 — Month 14)
- Formal offer/option
- Due diligence
- Close financing stack
- Acquire property
- Begin Phase 1 site improvements

## Key Metric: Days on Market
Every day the property sits unsold is a day we can organize. Every day a developer isn't in escrow is a win. Our single most important leading indicator is: **are we still in the game?**

Monitor the RMLS listing weekly. If it goes pending, we pivot to backup strategies (approach the buyer, negotiate community benefit agreement, etc.).
