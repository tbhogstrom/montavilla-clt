## Proposed Structure (15–20 minutes + Q&A)

### 1. Who We Are (3 min)
- MCLT = a coalition of Montavilla neighbors
- A community land trust holds land in trust for long-term community benefit
- We are NOT the farmers market, NOT a developer, NOT affiliated with the property owner
- We're outcome-open: the neighborhood decides what happens at 7700 SE Stark

### 2. Why This Matters Now (3 min)
- The property at 7700 SE Stark is listed for sale
- MFM's 20-year home is on land the market doesn't own or control
- A CLT is one way to give the community — including MFM — long-term security of tenure
- We're not predicting a crisis. We're preparing for uncertainty.

### 3. What We're Doing (5 min)
- PCEF Planning Grant ($200K) — technical analysis of the site: BPO, building feasibility, community engagement design
- MCLT applies, MCLT carries the work. MFM benefits from the analysis without the operational burden.
- Proud Ground TA relationship — expert guidance on CLT structure (reference status as of presentation date)
- Governance: MCLT has committed to reserving a board seat for MFM (not yet codified — Statton to confirm bylaws timeline)

### 4. What We've Learned from MFM's Own Work (3 min)
- Market Move Survey: 572 responses, 53% walk, 63% within a mile, #1 priority = proximity to home
- This data validates that the site matters — not just the institution
- We cite MFM's data with attribution, always. We do not republish without permission.

### 5. What We're Asking (2 min)
- NOT endorsement (unless the board wants to offer it)
- NOT money, partnership, MOU, or co-application
- We're asking: does the MFM board see value in this technical analysis being done? Are there questions the board wants the analysis to answer? Any concerns we should know about?

### 6. Q&A (open)
