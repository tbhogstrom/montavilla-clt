## 1. STRATEGIC POSITIONING: MCLT Relative to MFM

### The Core Principle

**MCLT works the land question. MFM runs the market. These are complementary, not overlapping.**

Lisa Hebert has publicly stated: *"They are not moving right now, and nobody has asked them to move."* Our job is to make sure that remains true — not by "saving" MFM (they don't need saving), but by ensuring the community has a voice in what happens to the land under their feet.

### Positioning Framework

| MCLT Is... | MCLT Is NOT... |
|---|---|
| A neighborhood coalition exploring community futures for 7700 SE Stark | The Montavilla Farmers Market's advocacy arm |
| Working to ensure community voice in land-use decisions | Claiming the market is in danger or needs rescuing |
| One of many stakeholders invested in the neighborhood | The authority on what should happen at this site |
| A potential long-term land steward | A competitor, partner, or affiliate of MFM |
| Interested in MFM's perspective as the primary tenant | Making decisions on MFM's behalf |

### The Key Reframe

**OLD:** "We're saving the farmers market lot."
**NEW:** "We're a coalition of neighbors working to make sure Montavilla has a voice in what happens at 7700 SE Stark. The farmers market is the heart of that site — we want to hear from Lisa and the MFM board about what matters most to them."

### Why This Works

1. **Respects MFM's autonomy.** Lisa runs a 501(c)(6) nonprofit with a board, ~100 vendors, and 20 years of institutional history. She does not need a months-old coalition telling her how to manage her organization.

2. **Aligns with Lisa's own framing.** She said: *"Nobody has asked them to move. But honestly, nobody knows what the future holds right now."* We're not predicting a crisis — we're preparing for uncertainty. That's responsible, not alarmist.

3. **Makes MFM a stakeholder, not a client.** In our reframed strategy (Apr 13), the coalition determines outcomes. MFM is the most important stakeholder in that process — but they are not the sole beneficiary. The neighborhood is.

4. **Avoids the credibility trap.** Reddit destroyed us for not having MFM's endorsement. Going to MFM with "we're saving you" will get us shown the door. Going with "we'd love your perspective" opens it.

## 5. ENDORSEMENT STRATEGY

### The Endorsement Ladder

Endorsement is not binary. It's a ladder, and we climb it slowly.

| Level | Description | What It Looks Like | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Level 0: Unaware** | MFM doesn't know MCLT exists | Current state for MFM org | Now |
| **Level 1: Aware** | Lisa/board know who we are and what we're doing | After intro email + coffee meeting | May 5-9 |
| **Level 2: Informed** | Lisa has been briefed on our process and is kept in the loop | After 2-3 meetings, shared info | May-June |
| **Level 3: Neutral-Positive** | Lisa/board are comfortable with our existence and approach | No objection to our work, willing to take calls | June-July |
| **Level 4: Informal Support** | Lisa mentions MCLT positively in conversations, refers interested parties | "Talk to those folks at MCLT" | July+ |
| **Level 5: Formal Letter of Support** | MFM board authorizes a letter supporting the PCEF planning grant | Written on MFM letterhead, signed by board chair or ED | Target: PCEF cycle 2 or USDA app |
| **Level 6: Public Endorsement** | MFM publicly states support for the MCLT process | Press, social media, joint event | Long-term aspiration |

### What an MFM Endorsement Looks Like (Compatible with Their Independence)

MFM will never say "MCLT is saving us." They don't need saving. A compatible endorsement looks like:

> *"The Montavilla Farmers Market supports community-driven processes that explore the long-term future of 7700 SE Stark Street. As a 20-year tenant of this site, we have a deep interest in its future and welcome the Montavilla Community Land Trust's commitment to centering community voice in land-use decisions. MFM remains an independent organization and any endorsement of the MCLT process does not imply a merger, partnership, or dependency."*

Key elements:
- Supports the *process*, not a specific outcome
- Positions MFM as a stakeholder with voice, not a beneficiary being rescued
- Explicitly preserves MFM's independence
- Does not commit MFM to any financial or organizational entanglement
- Does not use "save" language

### How Endorsements Feed Into PCEF

The PCEF planning grant application asks for evidence of community support and stakeholder engagement. An MFM letter of support would:
- Demonstrate that the primary site tenant supports the planning process
- Add institutional credibility (501(c)(6) with 20-year track record)
- Show that MCLT is doing genuine community engagement, not operating in a vacuum
- Differentiate us from the Montavilla Food Co-op failure (real organizational support vs. grassroots-only)

**Realistic timeline for PCEF:** We will NOT have an MFM letter of support for the May 27 PCEF deadline. That's 28 days away and we haven't made first contact. Do not promise this in the application. Instead:
- Reference the planned MFM outreach in the application narrative
- Note that MFM is a key stakeholder we will engage as part of the planning grant work
- If we get to Level 2 (Informed) by submission, mention that we've briefed MFM leadership

**For future grant cycles (USDA LFPP/FMPP June 5, or PCEF cycle 2):** A formal letter of support is realistic if the relationship develops well through May.

### Vendor Endorsements

Individual vendor testimonials and letters of support are valuable independent of MFM organizational endorsement:
- A quote from a 19-year vendor about what the site means to their farm business
- A letter from a new vendor about why they chose Montavilla
- These can go into PCEF and USDA applications as community support evidence
- Always get written permission before using any vendor's name or quote in grant materials

## 6. MESSAGE ALIGNMENT

### Talking Points the Team MUST Use

Every team member who interacts with MFM, vendors, or the public about this relationship uses these points:

1. **"We work the land question. They run the market."**
   The Montavilla Farmers Market is an independent 501(c)(6) nonprofit with 20 years of experience. We are a neighborhood coalition focused on community ownership of land. These are different missions that complement each other.

2. **"The neighborhood decides what happens here."**
   MCLT is not proposing a predetermined outcome. We're building a coalition to ensure Montavilla has a voice in the future of 7700 SE Stark — whatever that future looks like.

3. **"53% of shoppers walk to this market."**
   (Lisa's own data, from the Bridgetown Bites interview.) This market is a neighborhood asset, not a regional attraction. That's why the land underneath it matters to the people who live here.

4. **"The market is where you're face-to-face with the person whose hands are in the dirt."**
   (Lisa's words.) Direct-to-consumer agriculture is the core of what makes Montavilla special. Community land ownership can help ensure that continues.

5. **"Vendors say Montavilla is where they sustain their business."**
   (Lisa's words from the interview.) This market is economically vital for small farms. That's a fact worth protecting.

6. **"We want to hear from the people who've been here for 20 years."**
   We're the new ones. MFM was here first. We listen before we speak.

7. **"This is not a crisis. This is an opportunity."**
   The property is listed, but nobody has been asked to move. The question is: does the community want a say in what happens next? We think yes.

## 7. RISKS AND WHAT TO AVOID

### Risk 1: Looking Like Outsiders Leveraging MFM's Brand

**The danger:** MFM has 20 years of goodwill, ~100 vendors, thousands of weekly shoppers, EBT matching, and a Portland Events & Film Office grant. MCLT has $1,175 in pledges and a website that until today said "SAVE THIS LOT." If it looks like we're using MFM's name and reputation to boost our credibility, Lisa and the vendor community will shut us out permanently.

**Mitigation:**
- Never use MFM's name in our materials without written permission
- Never imply endorsement that doesn't exist
- Lead with what we bring (coalition process, land-use research, grant capacity), not with what MFM brings (brand, vendors, community trust)
- Build our own credibility independently — PCEF application, Proud Ground meeting, nonprofit formation — before asking MFM to associate with us

### Risk 2: Pushing the Crisis Frame

**The danger:** Our instinct (and our original messaging) is urgency: "the lot is listed NOW, a developer could close any time." Lisa is publicly saying "we are not moving, nobody has asked us to move." If we show up with crisis energy, we contradict her public statements and make her look either out of the loop or dishonest. Neither is acceptable.

**Mitigation:**
- Match Lisa's energy. She's celebrating 20 years, not panicking.
- Use "opportunity" framing, not "crisis" framing with MFM
- Save the urgency for internal team communications and grant timelines (PCEF deadline IS urgent — the MFM relationship is not)
- If asked directly about the listing, say: "Yes, the property is listed. Nobody's been asked to move. We think this is a good time for the community to start thinking about what it wants for this site long-term."

### Risk 3: Going Around MFM Leadership to Vendors

**The danger:** If we start approaching vendors with land-use questions before Lisa knows who we are, she will (rightly) see it as going behind her back. Vendors will tell Lisa. Lisa will be angry. Relationship destroyed before it begins.

**Mitigation:**
- Sequencing is everything. Lisa first. Always.
- Vendor outreach begins only after Lisa has been contacted and has responded
- If Lisa says "don't approach our vendors" — we don't approach their vendors
- Vendor interactions at the May 3 event are CUSTOMER interactions only

### Risk 4: Brand Confusion (MCLT ≠ MFM)

**The danger:** "Montavilla Community Land Trust" and "Montavilla Farmers Market" both start with "Montavilla" and are associated with the same site. People will confuse them. Media will blur the lines. A negative story about MCLT could splash onto MFM.

**Mitigation:**
- Always use full names, never abbreviations, in public-facing materials when referencing both organizations
- Include a clear disclaimer on our website: "The Montavilla Community Land Trust is an independent organization. We are not affiliated with the Montavilla Farmers Market."
- In any media interaction, proactively clarify the distinction
- Do not use market imagery on MCLT materials without permission

### Risk 5: MFM Survey Conflict

**The danger:** MFM has already put out a community survey about the market's future. If we launch our own competing survey or listening sessions, it duplicates effort and looks like we don't respect their process.

**Mitigation:**
- Ask Lisa about the survey in our first meeting — what are they learning?
- Do NOT launch a competing community survey about the market
- Our community engagement process (via PCEF) should focus on the LAND and NEIGHBORHOOD, not on the market specifically
- If possible, reference MFM's survey data (with permission) in our grant applications as existing community input

### Risk 6: Endorsement Overreach

**The danger:** We're desperate for legitimacy (Reddit told us so). The temptation will be to push for endorsement too fast, too early. One awkward ask and we lose the relationship.

**Mitigation:**
- The endorsement ladder (Section 5) is the pace. Level by level. No skipping.
- First meeting: relationship. Second meeting: information sharing. Third meeting: maybe discuss how to support each other. Endorsement comes when it comes.
- Internal rule: nobody asks Lisa or the MFM board for endorsement without Maven's explicit approval
