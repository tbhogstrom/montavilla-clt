## Step 3: Technical-Analysis Scope — What the Grant Funds

This is the substance of the PCEF application — what MCLT proposes to do with $200K.

### Proposed scope elements

1. **Broker Price Opinion (BPO)** — What is 7700 SE Stark actually worth? The listing at $5M is indefensible (comparable auto dealership with building listed at $2.25M). A formal BPO is essential for any negotiation and for PCEF's own assessment of project feasibility.

2. **Building-program feasibility** — What could the site physically support? CM2 zoning allows 4-story mixed-use. Options include: market pavilion, community hub, mixed-use with housing, or green space. Structural, architectural, and civil engineering assessment.

3. **Environmental review / gap analysis** — What environmental work has been done? What's needed? Tyler to request existing Phase I/II ESA from trust rep and listing agent post-Proud Ground (Step 4). PCEF funds the gap — whatever hasn't been done yet.

4. **Community engagement design** — Listening sessions, stakeholder mapping, scenario workshops. This is the process by which the neighborhood determines the outcome. MFM survey data (572 responses) is the starting evidence base; the PCEF process builds on it.

5. **Organizational development** — CLT governance (bylaws, tripartite board with MFM seat), nonprofit formation, ground lease template development. Proud Ground TA plugs in here.

6. **Project management** — MCLT staff/contractor to manage the scope. Absorbs the technical-planning load so MFM doesn't have to carry it (Lisa's capacity concern, directly addressed).

### What MFM receives without carrying

Lisa raised a real concern: MFM doesn't have staff bandwidth to drive this work. The Shoot the Moon scope is designed so MCLT applies, MCLT manages, MCLT carries the operational burden. MFM receives:
- The BPO (what the property is worth)
- The building feasibility study (what could be built)
- The environmental review (what the site's condition is)
- The community engagement results (what the neighborhood wants)

This is the analysis MFM's board needs before making any decision about the market's future on this site — funded by MCLT, delivered to MFM, without MFM's staff running the process.

## Step 4: Environmental Records Request — Post-Proud Ground

**Triggered by:** Proud Ground meeting outcome (Wed 5/6)
**What:** Request existing environmental work (Phase I ESA at minimum, possibly Phase II) from the trust representative and the listing agent.
**Why:** Any commercial property sale of this kind typically has environmental assessment already performed. Obtaining existing records (a) saves MCLT the cost of duplicating the work, and (b) shapes what environmental scope the PCEF grant needs to cover (gap analysis instead of full assessment).
**Sequencing:** Proud Ground first → trust rep + listing agent. The Proud Ground conversation shapes what kind of entity is making the request (MCLT alone? with TA disclosed? with fiscal sponsor?).
**Sensitivity:** The trust representative is the steering-group-only contact described in `meeting-notes/2026-04-29-stakeholder-intel.md`. The outreach itself is fine. Do not name or characterize him in any artifact that leaves the steering group.

## Fallback: If Shoot the Moon Fails

If MCLT cannot secure a fiscal sponsor by ~May 10, the May 27 PCEF window closes. Fallback options:

1. **USDA LFPP + FMPP — deadline June 5 (31 days).** $75K–$350K combined. Still requires fiscal sponsor but gives 9 more days to find one. Different framing (direct-market agriculture, market development) but overlapping scope.

2. **PCEF Cycle 2** — next cycle likely 12+ months away. Not acceptable as a plan but real as a backstop. The coalition work, MFM relationship, and Proud Ground TA continue regardless.

3. **Collins Foundation / Meyer Memorial Trust / Metro Placemaking** — rolling or later deadlines. Smaller amounts. Not a substitute for PCEF's $200K planning grant.

**Bottom line:** Shoot the Moon for PCEF May 27 is the highest-value, highest-urgency grant window MCLT has. If it closes, the campaign doesn't end, but the pace drops dramatically and the property risk increases.
